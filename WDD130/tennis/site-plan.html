Overview

Purpose

A professional tennis coach needs a website to provide information about his services as well as provide his qualifications and contact information to potential clients. So, as a web designer, I want to make a better website for you. I know that you are a tennis instructor and you have a small online presence through social media but you really should have a personal website for your company. Everyone should be able to search your name and find your website with information about your services. Links to your social media, especially Instagram and Facebook should increase your business.

Audience

The audience is parents, children, high school coaches, anyone interested in tennis instruction. Very often, parents and coaches are looking for extra training for their children and a private coach is just the solution. Adults who may want to improve their game also might want a private instructor. Even professionals pay for private instruction. Everyone can improve. It should be easy to type in "tennis coach" to a search engine and find a coach near you.

Typography

Heading Font: Roboto

I want the font to be professional and serious, no handwriting type or comic sans for this site! Roboto is a little heavier than the font I'm using for the paragraphs, appropriate for the headings. It's easy to read and simple, perfect so as not to distract the user from the information provided.

Paragraph Font: Ubuntu

This is less intense than the Roboto used in the headings. It's simple and direct and very easy to read.

About Tennis--A History

Tennis is believed to have originated in France among French monks who played a crude handball against monastery walls. It was originally called jeu de paume or “game of the palm” because they hit the ball with their hands. It was probably painful to repeatedly hit the balls with their hands, so after trying a glove, eventually the monks graduated to using a racket. The balls were made of hair, wool or cork wrapped in string or cloth. The French nobility learned the game from the monks and it spread to England. That early game differed from the “lawn tennis” we play today, it’s called “real tennis” and is played on an indoor court. Tennis’ popularity waned in the 1700’s until it enjoyed a revival in 1850 with the invention of rubber. That is when the outdoor tennis game became possible. Of course, outdoor games couldn’t use the walls and whole new set of rules were created. In 1874, London Major Walter Wingfield patented equipment for a game very similar to the tennis we play today. Within a few years, his equipment had been sold and installed in the US, Russia, India, Canada and China.

What’s the deal with the love? Anytime there is a score of zero it is referred to as love. There are a few theories as to why. One theory is the French expression for egg-l’oeuf. A zero looks sort of like an egg and apparently some people beleive the pronunciation of l’oeuf got butchered so much over the years it became love.

Another theory is that love really just means for love of the game. When you have zero points, the only thing keeping you playing is the love of the game.
